About BDC Intelligence

We exist to make private credit transparent, comprehensible, and worth the time of anyone trying to understand it.

Private credit has quietly become one of the largest pools of capital in the world. The data describing it has not kept up. We are trying to close that gap.

Our mission

Take the fragmented, buried, and intentionally opaque data inside BDC filings and turn it into something a credit analyst can read in five minutes.

Our vision

A world where the only excuse for not knowing what is happening in private credit is the willingness to look. We want to remove every other excuse.

Why this exists

Business development companies have become the most important publicly visible window into US private credit. They originate, hold, and disclose loans to sponsor backed borrowers that would otherwise be invisible to public markets. Yet the data is scattered across hundreds of SEC filings, each formatted differently, none of it queryable, almost none of it cross comparable.

The result is that credit analysts spend more time reformatting than analyzing. A question as basic as which BDCs hold this borrower, and at what marks? can take an afternoon. How has non accrual percentage shifted across the 2021 to 2022 vintage cohort? requires building a spreadsheet from scratch every quarter.

We built BDC Intelligence because the credit cycle is turning, the public BDC universe has tripled in five years, and the analytical infrastructure to make sense of it has not.

Coverage spans traded BDCs from the largest sponsor affiliated names (ARCC, OBDC, FSK, MAIN, BBDC) to small internally managed funds, plus public non traded BDCs including BCRED, HLEND, OTF, and the newer wave of perpetual private credit vehicles. Every fund is identified by its SEC CIK so the lineage is always verifiable back to the primary filing.

How we work

BDC Intelligence runs on one strict principle. Every data point traces back to a primary source on SEC.gov. No proprietary feeds. No scraped third party aggregators. When a number appears on this site, the underlying 8 K, 10 Q, or 10 K is one click away.

Quarterly fundamentals

NAV per share, NII, non accrual percentage, leverage, weighted average yield, portfolio composition, plus fifty other metrics extracted from 10 Q and 10 K filings. Updated quarterly as funds report.

Schedule of Investments

Line item portfolio data. Every borrower, every tranche, every mark. Extracted from each fund's SOI exhibit. Indexed to a unified borrower registry that surfaces cross fund overlap.

Material event feed

All 8 K filings since January 2020. Headlines written from the actual press release exhibits where available. Falls back to SEC item code labels for administrative filings. Every item links to the filing.

Thesis screening

Thirty five pre built credit theses covering distress signals, structural risks, and forward indicators. Each runs live against current data. Thresholds adjust on the fly. Sparkline shows trajectory across the last eight quarters.

What we are not

Not a substitute for primary diligence. Not a substitute for institutional data providers like Bloomberg, Reorg, or PitchBook. This is an open data complement. Faster to scan, easier to query, more transparent about its sources. Where the data is sparse, the page says so. Where a metric is estimated, the methodology is named. Where coverage is incomplete, the gaps are visible.
