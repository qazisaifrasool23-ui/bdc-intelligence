Public market restructurings. The deals that taught the market what cov lite actually meant.

These are the famous moments where modern indenture loopholes were tested and either survived or collapsed in court. Anyone who lends in the syndicated market needs to know these stories. Every modern indenture review traces its DNA to one of these decisions.

Drop down · 2017
J Crew Group
Sponsor: TPG, Leonard GreenOutcome: Filed 2020
The transaction that named the genre. J Crew moved its IP to an unrestricted subsidiary and raised $300M of new debt against it, leaving the existing TLB lenders with claims on the empty operating business. The structure held in court. The case became the template for every later drop down.
Suggested for coverage
Uptier · 2020
Serta Simmons Bedding
Sponsor: AdventOutcome: Litigation ongoing
Serta gave a subset of its first lien lenders the right to swap into a new super priority tranche, leaving the rest of the first lien holders subordinated. The minority lenders are still in court arguing this was illegal. Defines the modern uptier exchange and the limits of "open market purchase" language.
Suggested for coverage
Chapter 11 · 2020
Hertz Global Holdings
Type: Retail bankruptcyOutcome: Equity recovered
The first major bankruptcy where retail equity holders ended up better off than most senior creditors. Pandemic collapse, surprise rental car shortage, then unusual recovery. A case study in how bankruptcy outcomes can defy textbook predictions when the underlying business unexpectedly turns.
Suggested for coverage
Chapter 11 · 2023
Envision Healthcare
Sponsor: KKROutcome: Filed May 2023
The defining health services bankruptcy of the post COVID cycle. $7B+ of debt, complex out of court attempts, eventual Chapter 11. The first lien holders took control of the equity; existing equity wiped out. A template for how surprise medical billing regulation hit credit.
Suggested for coverage

Private credit, defining deals. Where the asset class showed what it could and could not do.

These are private credit transactions that shaped the modern direct lending landscape. Some are landmarks of scale. Others are workouts that revealed how the asset class actually behaves in stress. All of them are visible to BDC analysts via the SOI exhibits of the funds involved.

Mega direct loan · 2021
Stripe acquisition financing (Bridge)
Lenders: Multiple directSize: ~$1B
One of the earliest examples of direct lenders winning a "trophy" deal away from the syndicated market for a high quality fintech borrower. Marked the moment institutional appetite for direct lending hit critical mass for $1B+ tickets. Set pricing benchmarks for similar later deals.
Suggested for coverage
Unitranche · 2022
Athenahealth take private
Sponsor: Bain, Hellman & FriedmanSize: $17B EV
The largest unitranche financing in private credit history. Provided by a club of direct lenders including Ares, Blackstone, Sixth Street, others. Demonstrated that private credit could now finance public to private transactions at scale historically reserved for the syndicated market.
Suggested for coverage
Distressed / workout · 2024
Pluralsight
Sponsor: Vista EquityLenders: Direct lenders
A direct lending workout where Vista Equity walked away from a software company while the direct lenders (Ares, Blue Owl, Goldman, others) absorbed the company. The first highly visible case where a top tier sponsor handed the keys back, and the direct lenders had to operate the business directly. Critical case study in 2026 cycle dynamics.
Suggested for coverage
Recap · 2023
Veritas Capital portfolio companies
Sponsor: Veritas CapitalLender: Multiple BDCs
A sequence of dividend recap deals where Veritas extracted equity from defense and gov tech portfolio companies through layered direct loans. Illustrates how sponsors use the BDC channel to recap portfolio companies without needing public market access. Shows up in multiple BDC SOIs.
Suggested for coverage

BDC milestones. The corporate events that shaped the BDC universe itself.

Sometimes the most important credit story is about a BDC, not a borrower. Mergers, IPOs, dividend cuts, and new vehicle launches change the supply and demand dynamics of the entire BDC ecosystem. These are the BDC level events worth studying as case examples.

BDC merger · 2024
Owl Rock and Capital Corp merger (OBDC)
Type: Affiliated mergerResult: $13B+ BDC
The 2024 combination of two affiliated Owl Rock BDCs into a single $13B+ vehicle (OBDC). One of the largest BDC consolidations on record, set the template for size driven consolidation across the universe. Tells the story of why "platform scale" matters in modern BDC management.
Suggested for coverage
Non traded launch · 2022
BCRED (Blackstone Private Credit Fund)
Manager: BlackstoneAUM: $50B+ in 2025
Possibly the most successful non traded BDC launch in history. Scaled from launch to $50B AUM in roughly four years. Defines what a modern wealth channel BDC looks like in terms of fee economics, distribution mechanics, and quarterly tender behavior.
Suggested for coverage
Dividend cut · 2024
FS KKR (FSK) repositioning
Type: Multi quarter cutOutcome: Stock response
FSK reduced its dividend through a series of steps in 2024 to align distributions with net investment income. A textbook case of how dividend cuts get telegraphed, executed, and absorbed by the market. Useful for understanding the dividend coverage signal in the screener.
Suggested for coverage
IPO · 2025
Blue Owl Technology Income Corp
Manager: Blue OwlType: Spinoff listing
A non traded BDC that converted to listed status via IPO, an increasingly common pathway for non traded vehicles seeking better liquidity. Case study in the structural premium / discount dynamic of going from NAV based to market based pricing.

How BDCs actually invest. Reference layer.

Before any deal teardown, it helps to know the vehicles a BDC uses to put money to work. There are roughly seven distinct investment forms. Each behaves differently in stress and shows up differently in the SOI exhibit.

Direct first lien
Senior secured loans originated bilaterally with the borrower. The core asset of most BDCs. Senior position in the cap stack, first claim on collateral.
Second lien and sub
Subordinated debt sitting below the first lien. Higher yield (12 to 15 percent) in exchange for lower recovery in default. Common in larger LBOs.
Unitranche
Blended first and second lien in a single tranche. Single lender or small club. Borrower simplicity, lender blended yield. The growth product of the last decade.
Equity and warrants
Direct equity stakes or warrant kickers attached to debt. Often acquired in workouts as part of restructured positions. Lumpy returns, mark to market.
JV / SLF structures
Off balance sheet vehicles co invested with one or two partners. Hold loans the BDC originated. Levered inside the JV for higher yield on the BDC's invested capital.
Asset backed lending
Loans collateralized by specific identifiable assets (equipment, receivables, real estate). Different recovery dynamics than corporate loans. Growing share of newer BDCs.
NAV loans
Lending against the NAV of a private equity or private credit portfolio. Newer product, increasingly visible. Highly bespoke. Worth a primer of its own at some point.
Syndicated participations
Some BDCs (especially older traded ones) participate in syndicated TLBs alongside their direct lending positions. Less common but still meaningful in 5 to 10 percent of portfolios.
Specialty (royalties, etc)
Niche structures (royalty backed loans, pharmaceutical financing, music royalty rights). A handful of BDCs specialize. Usually a small portfolio percentage even for those.
