The Thesis

Credit moves slower than equity — until it doesn't.

BDCs and private credit funds disclose more about middle-market lending than any other source in the public domain. Every quarter, ~170 funds publish hundreds of line items each: who they lent to, at what spread, at what mark, on what terms.

That disclosure sits inside 10-Qs and 10-Ks that nobody reads end-to-end. The signal is there — concentration risk, mark drift, sector rotation, the early tells of a distressed cycle — but it's buried in unstructured PDFs and inconsistent iXBRL filings. By the time it surfaces in trade press or sell-side research, the move has happened.

Chewbacca reads all of it. Every filing, every quarter, every line. It structures the Schedule of Investments into queryable data, tracks every position across every quarter, and tells you what changed — by fund, by sector, by sponsor, by individual borrower — before the market notices.

It's not a chatbot. It's an analyst. The difference is whether you wait for an answer, or wake up to one.
