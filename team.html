About BDC Intelligence
BDC Intelligence is an independent research platform covering the full US Business Development Company universe — 68 SEC-registered funds representing $228B in private credit assets.
We built this because the data exists — scattered across thousands of SEC filings — but the tools to make sense of it at scale do not. Every BDC files quarterly reports with the SEC. We extract, standardise, and score that data using the same analytical framework across all 68 funds.
The result: the first platform where a retail investor and a credit analyst can look at the same fund through the same data model — and draw their own conclusions.

EQS Methodology
Earnings Quality Score · Formula, dimensions, and data sources
The Formula
EQS = D1×0.35 + D2×0.25 + D3×0.20 + D4×0.20
Each dimension is scored 1-5. The composite score ranges from 1 (worst) to 5 (best). A score above 4.0 indicates strong earnings quality. Below 3.0 warrants scrutiny.
Academic Grounding
The EQS framework draws on Sloan (1996) on accrual-based earnings quality, Dechow et al. (2010) on earnings quality measurement, Richardson et al. (2005) on accruals and future returns, KBRA's BDC credit framework, and Moody's (2021) private credit methodology. It is adapted for the specific characteristics of BDC financial reporting.
D1 — Cash Realisation
35%
CashInc% = (TII - PIK) / TII
5 = above 90%
4 = 80-90%
3 = 70-80%
2 = 60-70%
1 = below 60%
Measures what fraction of total investment income is received in cash. High PIK = low score. Based on Sloan (1996) accrual quality framework.
D2 — NII Coverage
25%
Coverage = NII / DPS
5 = above 1.20x
4 = 1.10-1.20x
3 = 1.00-1.10x
2 = 0.90-1.00x
1 = below 0.90x
Distribution sustainability. A fund paying more than it earns is drawing down capital. The most direct signal of dividend risk.
D3 — PIK Trend
20%
Direction over trailing 4 quarters
5 = declining 3+ qtrs
3 = flat/mixed
1 = rising 3+ qtrs
Trend matters more than level. Rising PIK signals deteriorating borrower health. Falling PIK signals improving cash generation.
D4 — Credit Quality
20%
Non-Accrual % vs 2.5% peer median
5 = below 1.0%
4 = 1.0-2.0%
3 = 2.0-3.0%
2 = 3.0-4.5%
1 = above 4.5%
Non-accrual rate relative to the 2.5% peer benchmark. Funds significantly above peer median are pricing in elevated credit risk.
Data Sources
Where every number comes from
SEC EDGAR
Primary source. All 10-K and 10-Q filings for all 68 funds. Structured data extracted via XBRL (iXBRL) tags standardised across filings. Covers: NAV, NII, total investment income, management fees, interest expense, leverage, distributions.
Coverage: 1,358 quarter-records
10-Q Supplemental
Non-accrual rates and PIK income percentages are extracted from the supplemental schedules in quarterly reports. These are not standardised in XBRL — they require text extraction and pattern matching across fund-specific formats.
Coverage: 27 funds (PIK) · 19 funds (NA%)
Market Data
Price-to-NAV ratios for traded BDCs calculated from market prices against reported NAV per share. For non-traded funds, secondary market pricing (LODAS, CAIS) is not yet integrated — NAV is self-reported by the fund.
Traded BDCs only · Non-traded: self-reported NAV
